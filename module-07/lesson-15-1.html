Energy Transformations in Organisms
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Your body doesn't create energy out of nothing. It transforms energy that already existed into a form it can use.
Energy doesn't just get "used up," it changes form. Chemical energy stored in food becomes movement, heat, and the energy that powers your thinking, in the same way a car engine converts chemical energy in gasoline into motion and heat.

📚 PART 1 · CHEMICAL ENERGY BECOMES EVERYTHING ELSE

Food stores chemical energy in the bonds between its atoms. Digesting and metabolizing food is a chemical change: your body breaks some of those bonds, which actually takes a small amount of energy, and rearranges the atoms into new molecules by forming new bonds. Forming those new bonds releases more energy than it took to break the old ones, and that net leftover energy is what your body actually gets to use. That released energy then transforms into other forms: kinetic energy (movement), thermal energy (body heat), and the energy that powers thinking and cell repair. None of this energy is created from scratch, and none of it disappears either, exactly like the Law of Conservation of Mass and Energy predicts.
