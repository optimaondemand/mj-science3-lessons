The Law of Conservation of Mass
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
If you burn a log and weigh the ashes, they weigh less than the log did. Did matter just disappear?
This is the last big idea of Unit 2, and it ties everything together: matter is never created or destroyed in a physical or chemical change, it just changes form. When something seems to "lose mass," the missing matter almost always went somewhere you didn't measure.

📚 PART 1 · THE MISSING MASS ISN'T MISSING

The Law of Conservation of Mass says the total mass before a change exactly equals the total mass after, as long as you account for everything, including gases that escape. Burning wood releases mass as invisible gases (mostly carbon dioxide and water vapor) into the air. If you burned the wood in a sealed container and weighed the whole sealed container, including the gas trapped inside, the mass wouldn't have changed at all.

⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE
Seeing a scale reading go down after a reaction does not mean mass was destroyed. It almost always means some of the mass left the container as a gas you didn't weigh. This is exactly why your Fizzing Reaction assignment's bubbles matter, they're carrying mass away as CO₂ gas.
