Cellular Respiration: Releasing the Energy
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Photosynthesis builds food. Cellular respiration is how you (and the plant itself) break it back down to get the energy out.
Almost every cell in almost every living thing, including plant cells, runs cellular respiration constantly. Today you learn the process, and then you put it side by side with photosynthesis to see just how perfectly the two mirror each other.

📚 PART 1 · THE MIRROR PROCESS

Cellular respiration takes glucose and oxygen as inputs, and releases carbon dioxide, water, usable cellular energy, and heat as outputs. Notice something important: photosynthesis and cellular respiration are complementary processes, not an exact mirror image reversed in every detail. Photosynthesis stores light energy into glucose's chemical bonds; cellular respiration unlocks much of that stored energy back out as usable cellular energy, but some of it is always released as heat instead, energy that doesn't get stored back into anything and can't be perfectly recovered. The matter (carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen atoms) cycles between the two processes far more completely than the energy does.
